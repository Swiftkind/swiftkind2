When the U.S. places tariffs on China’s imports, companies must pay an additional tax on all products that they buy from that country. In theory, there are different possible effects of this tax. Importers could pass the full burden of the tax onto consumers, in the form of higher prices; importers could pass some of the burden onto consumers and absorb some of the burden themselves (reducing their profit margins); or exporting Chinese firms, faced with lower demand from U.S. consumers, could be forced to reduce their prices, bearing some of the burden themselves.
